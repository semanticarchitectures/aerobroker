▸ Design notes (asymmetric-info broker model)
The wedge

Each party tells the broker things they wouldn't tell the other side. The broker is the only entity holding both ledgers — it can find matches and propose terms that neither MLS search nor either side's agent could.

Why this is hard for a marketplace

Marketplaces work by exposing inventory and price. This model deliberately doesn't — it keeps the most decision-relevant info private. The conversation is the product; the broker's value is steward­ship, not inventory.

Behavioral signals

The intent-to-buy gauge moves with revealed and inferred behavior: dwell time on listings, repeat visits, asked questions, sent-to-spouse, home-tour requests. These never go to sellers but inform the broker's match scoring.

Open design questions
  • How should the broker disclose that it knows something private without revealing what?
  • Fiduciary status — agent-of-buyer, agent-of-seller, or dual-agent? US laws vary by state and matter a lot here.
  • Compensation: % of savings vs. retainer vs. spread on price-discovery.
  • What's the audit trail when a deal closes — proving the broker handled confidences fairly?
